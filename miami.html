Brand Style Guide

Professional design standards for B2B reseller marketplace communications

Brand Foundation

Mission: To provide resellers with a reliable source of high-quality, brand-name products at exceptional clearance and wholesale prices, enabling them to grow their businesses profitably.
Target Audience: B2B resellers, bulk buyers, liquidation businesses, and e-commerce entrepreneurs looking for high-margin inventory.
Platform Scale: 1200+ premium brands across 130+ categories - from electronics and fashion to home goods and industrial equipment. Our vast inventory requires flexible design systems that can showcase diversity while maintaining professional consistency.

Brand Personality

Minimal & Sleek

Clean, uncluttered designs that let products shine. No visual noise - just efficient, conversion-focused layouts.

Profit-Oriented

Every design element serves business goals. Focus on margins, ROI, and actionable opportunities for resellers.

Results-Driven

High-conversion design for busy professionals. Speed, clarity, and immediate value recognition are paramount.

Flexible & Scalable

Adaptable design system that works across 1200+ brands and 130+ categories while maintaining consistency.

Design Philosophy: Minimal & Conversion-Focused

The Miamilots Approach: Less is more. Our designs serve one primary purpose - helping busy professionals quickly identify profitable opportunities across our vast inventory of 1200+ brands and 130+ categories.

Core Design Principles

Speed Over Sophistication

Professionals need instant clarity. Prioritize immediate comprehension over decorative elements.

White Space as Tool

Generous spacing reduces cognitive load and increases focus on key profit metrics and CTAs.

Data-Driven Hierarchy

Price, quantity, brand, and margin data should be immediately scannable in order of business importance.

Flexible Brand Expression

System must accommodate everything from luxury fashion to industrial equipment while maintaining coherence.

Design Freedom Guidelines

  • Explore creative layouts within the established color and typography system
  • Adapt messaging tone to match category context (tech vs fashion vs industrial)
  • Use product imagery creatively while maintaining white background consistency
  • Experiment with grid systems and information hierarchy
  • Test bold approaches that maintain professional credibility
  • Balance creativity with conversion optimization

Category-Specific Design Systems

With 130+ categories serving different retailer types, designs must target specific merchant audiences. Categories are grouped by retailer similarity to create cohesive visual approaches.

Retailer-Focused Category Groups

Electronics & Tech Retailers

Categories: Electronics & Appliances, Computers/Hardware, Video Games, Mobile Devices
Visual Elements: Clean geometric layouts, technical icons, precise typography
Colors: Blue (#337AB7), Silver (#A0AEC0), White backgrounds
Typography: Roboto Medium/Bold, tabular numbers for specs
Effects: Subtle drop shadows on product images only

Fashion & Lifestyle Retailers

Categories: Clothing/Fashion, Shoes, Personal Care/Beauty
Visual Elements: Elegant spacing, lifestyle context, brand-forward presentation
Colors: Teal (#0D8082), Soft grays (#F8FAFC), Accent pink (#ff2065)
Typography: Roboto Light for elegance, Bold for brand names
Effects: Soft gradients allowed, no harsh shadows

Home & Family Retailers

Categories: Home & Garden, Toys & Baby, Books/Movies/Music
Visual Elements: Practical layouts, family-friendly, organized grids
Colors: Warm teal (#0D8082), Earth tones (#4A5568), Light backgrounds
Typography: Roboto Regular, clear hierarchy for families
Effects: Rounded corners acceptable, soft backgrounds

Sports & Outdoor Retailers

Categories: Sports & Outdoors, Automotive & Industrial
Visual Elements: Dynamic angles, action-oriented, durability focus
Colors: Forest green (#2D5A27), Ocean blue (#1E3A8A), Earth brown (#8B4513)
Typography: Roboto Bold, strong contrast for readability
Effects: Angular elements, bold shadows permitted

Professional & Office Retailers

Categories: Office & School Supplies, Pet Products
Visual Elements: Professional layouts, utility-focused, efficiency emphasis
Colors: Corporate blue (#1A202C), Professional gray (#4A5568), Clean white
Typography: Roboto Medium, business-appropriate hierarchy
Effects: Minimal effects, clean lines only

Design Freedom: Within each group, adapt visual elements to match retailer expectations while maintaining core Miamilots brand elements (typography, primary teal, professional approach).

Typography

Professional Typography Standards

Required Font: Roboto (Google Fonts) - No exceptions
Rationale: Optimized for digital screens, excellent readability across all devices, professional appearance, supports multiple weights and languages.
‚ùå Typography Violations:
  • Cursive/Script fonts (Pacifico, Dancing Script, etc.) - Poor mobile readability
  • Decorative fonts (Impact, Brush Script, etc.) - Unprofessional for B2B
  • Serif fonts for body text (Times New Roman, Georgia) - Harder to read on screens
  • Comic Sans or similar - Destroys professional credibility
  • Multiple font mixing - Creates visual chaos and brand inconsistency

Exception: Logo gradient treatment only - no other gradient text allowed.

H1 - Main Heading (28px, Bold)
H2 - Section Title (22px, Bold)
H3 - Sub-heading (18px, Medium)
Body Text - Product descriptions and general content (14px, Regular)
Price Text - $299.99 (18px, Bold, Teal)
Labels/Badges - Used, 50 pcs (12px, Regular)

Banner & Campaign Design Standards

High-Impact Marketing: Banners and campaigns must work across diverse product categories while maintaining conversion focus. With our scale of 1200+ brands, designs need flexibility without sacrificing effectiveness.

Campaign Design Flexibility

Highlight specific premium brands (Apple, Nike, Samsung). Use brand colors as accents while maintaining Miamilots primary palette.

Seasonal Opportunities

Adapt messaging for peak selling seasons. Back-to-school, holiday inventory, summer clearance - tailor value propositions.

Inventory-Driven Design

Let available inventory inspire creative direction. Highlight unique lots, bulk opportunities, or limited-time offerings.
