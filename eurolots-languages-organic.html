Executive Summary: A Data‑Driven SEO Plan to Focus on Revenue‑Driving Pages

We align indexing and crawl budget with proven buyer intent to grow organic revenue for wholesale and liquidation traffic.

The Problem

Our site generates millions of URLs, yet search traffic is highly concentrated. Google spends significant crawl budget on pages that receive zero clicks, especially untranslated product pages in non‑English languages. This dilutes SEO performance and slows ranking on our most valuable pages.

The Analysis

We segmented Google Search Console clicks by language and page type:

  • Main Pages Homepage and company information
  • List Pages Categories, Brands, Newest, Lots
  • Product Pages Individual item pages

Key Findings

  • Traffic concentration Bulgarian, English, and French deliver 92% of clicks.
  • Product page gap Outside English, product pages across other languages deliver negligible traffic despite massive index footprint.
  • Bulgarian strength BG leads on Main and List pages, signaling strong local engagement.
Business impact Concentrate indexing on revenue‑driving languages and page types to accelerate discovery and rankings for buyers searching for clothing wholesale, electronics wholesale, and appliances wholesale.

The Business Case

We are not losing valuable traffic. We are cleaning up our index footprint to match real buyer behavior. This improves crawl efficiency, Core Web Vitals, and ranking velocity on pages that drive revenue from wholesale and liquidation buyers.

KEEP INDEXRetain visibility for proven demand
APPLY NOINDEXRemove low‑value URLs from the index

Implementation Plan

  1. Robots Meta Rules Roll out conditional noindex, follow on Tier 2 and Tier 3 List pages and on all non‑EN product pages. Preserve internal linking equity with follow.
  2. XML Sitemaps Publish language tier sitemaps. Include only Tier 1 Main and List pages plus EN product pages.
  3. Canonical Hygiene Standardize canonicals and URL normalization to avoid duplicates in multilingual contexts.
  4. Translations Prioritize high‑intent List pages for BG, EN, FR. Defer mass product translations until they show traction.
  5. Monitoring Track impressions, clicks, indexed pages, and crawl stats weekly. Guardrail with custom alerts.

Success Metrics (60–90 days)

  • ↓ Indexed pages for low‑value segments
  • ↑ Crawl rate and average position for Tier 1 List pages
  • ↑ Click share on BG, EN, FR List and Main pages
  • Stable or ↑ conversions from organic sessions
