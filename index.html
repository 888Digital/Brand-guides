🎯 The 3-Second Deal Rule

Our data proves our best customers are scanners, not readers. You have 3 seconds to communicate the core opportunity. Your design must instantly answer:

1. What is the DEAL TYPE? - Fixed Price, Promo, or Negotiation?
2. What is the MAIN TARGET? - A specific Brand or a Category?
3. What is the PRODUCT? - Individual Products, a Lot, or a Feature?

6 Design Don'ts: The Readability Killers

Any design choice that slows down a reseller's ability to scan for data is a conversion killer. The following practices are strictly forbidden because they violate the "3-Second Deal Rule."

🚫 Cursive & Overly-Styled Fonts

Why it fails: Resellers' brains are optimized to scan block text for numbers and keywords, not to decipher calligraphy. It instantly fails the 3-second test.

🚫 Text on Busy Backgrounds

Why it fails: If the price or brand name doesn't have a clear, high-contrast background, it gets lost. A pattern or busy photo behind critical data makes it unreadable.

🚫 Text in Shapes or on a Path

Why it fails: Text in circles, on a diagonal, or following a curve forces the user to work to read it. This adds seconds to the scan time and should be avoided for all critical information.

🚫 Vague Headlines & CTAs

Why it fails: "Amazing Summer Deals!" tells a reseller nothing. "70% Off All Electronics Lots" tells them everything. Always prioritize the specific deal over a catchy but empty slogan.

7 Crafting High-Conversion Subject Lines

A great banner is useless if the email is never opened. The subject line is the most important part of the email campaign. Follow these rules.

✓ Formula: [Value] + [Product] + [Urgency/Qualifier]

  • Lead with the DEAL. Use numbers, percentages %, and currency symbols $ first.
  • Be SPECIFIC about the Brand or Category.
  • Add a REASON to act now or name of the event e.g., "48-Hour Clearance", "NJ Overload", "Weekend Clearance", "Labor Day Promo".
  • FRONT-LOAD the most important info. Assume it will be cut off on mobile.
  • 50% OFF - Electronics & Apparel Lots - 48-Hour Clearance
  • NEGOTIATION SALE - All FBA Inventory - Labor Day Promo
  • $5.00 MEGA Blowout - The North Face • Tory Burch • Nike...

✗ Don't Use Vague, Retail-Style Language

  • Avoid generic, "fluffy" phrases that hide the value.
  • Never bury the main offer at the end of the subject line.
  • Don't be clever if it sacrifices clarity.
  • Don't Miss Out on Our Amazing September Savings!
  • Celebrate Fall With Miamilots Deals
  • Find the Best Prices in our Labor Day Sale: 50% OFF

8 The Quality Control Process

Follow these two steps before submitting any creative for review.

Step 1: The 5-Point Pre-Flight Check

  • Is the Deal Type Promo, Negotiation, etc. instantly clear?
  • Does the design obey the Deal Hierarchy & Font System?
  • Is the Reason/Qualifier e.g., "1s & 2s" clear and effective?
  • Is the Call-to-Action correct for the deal type? "Buy Now" vs. "Make Offer"
  • Is every word, number, and brand name 100% accurate and spell-checked?

Step 2: LLM-Powered Self-Assessment

You are a professional reseller scanning Miamilots visuals. You have 3 seconds to decide if this is worth your time. Can you instantly answer: Can I read this? • Is the text clear and easy to scan? • Is anything hard to read due to fonts, colors, or busy backgrounds? What's the opportunity? • What's the deal? (Discount, Price, Negotiation, New Feature) • What am I buying? (Specific brands, categories, or lot types) • Why should I act now? (Limited time, special condition, exclusive access) What do I do next? • Is there a clear button or action? • Does it make sense for this type of deal? OUTPUT: ✓ YES or ✗ NO for each question Brief list of what's confusing or missing.
